Workshop Guide

Cognitive-Narrative

A facilitated team session to explore and strengthen Cognitive-Narrative resilience

⏱ 60–90 minutes 👥 5–25 participants 🎯 Team development

Overview

Cognitive-Narrative resilience is the ability to construct empowering stories about challenges and opportunities. Teams with strong cognitive resilience reframe setbacks constructively, maintain growth-oriented narratives, and think flexibly in the face of uncertainty.

This guide provides a structured facilitation framework including discussion prompts, activities, and reflection questions. Adapt the timing and sequence to suit your team's needs.

Facilitator note: Create a psychologically safe space before starting. Remind participants that sharing is always voluntary and that there are no right or wrong answers.

Discussion Prompts

Choose 3–5 prompts that feel most relevant to your team. Allow 5–8 minutes per prompt for open discussion.

  1. What stories do we tell ourselves about our challenges — are they empowering or limiting?
  2. When we face a setback, how do we typically make sense of what happened?
  3. What assumptions do we hold that might be limiting our potential as a team?
  4. How do we share knowledge and learning so the whole team benefits?
  5. What would change if we adopted a "not yet" mindset toward our struggles?
  6. Can you think of a time our team turned a failure into a valuable lesson?
  7. What narratives do we want to tell about who we are as a team?

Team Activities

Select one or two activities depending on your available time.

  • Reframe Challenge (20 min): Each person shares a current challenge. The team offers 3 alternative ways to frame or interpret it. Discuss which framings feel most resourceful.
  • Learning Harvest (15 min): Reflect on a recent team setback. Identify: What did we learn? What would we do differently? What unexpected strength did we discover?
  • Assumptions Audit (20 min): Brainstorm assumptions you hold about your team, your work, or your capabilities. Circle any that might be limiting.

Reflection Questions

Close the session with individual reflection (5 min silent writing) followed by brief sharing.

  • What story about your team's capability do you want to strengthen?
  • What's one limiting belief or assumption you're willing to let go of?
  • How will you bring a "reframe" mindset into your next challenge?

Next Steps

After the workshop, capture commitments and schedule a follow-up check-in:

  • Each person identifies one concrete action they'll take before the next check-in.
  • The team agrees on one shared practice to implement together.
  • Schedule a 15-minute check-in in 2–4 weeks to review progress.
  • Consider reassessing your team's resilience profile in 30 days to track growth.

Track progress: Return to your Team Dashboard after your next assessment cycle to see how your scores have shifted.
