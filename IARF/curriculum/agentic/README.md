# Agentic-Generative Dimension Curriculum

> *Agency is the belief that your actions matter — and the practice of acting as if they do.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Agentic-Generative** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to take purposeful action, maintain self-efficacy, and generate forward momentum — even in the face of adversity, uncertainty, or loss of control.

People with strong agentic resilience:
- Set goals and pursue them even under stress
- Believe their actions can make a difference
- Find ways to contribute and create, even in constrained circumstances
- Maintain a sense of purpose and direction through difficulty

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Identify and articulate their personal values and how they relate to action
- Set small, achievable goals aligned with their values
- Build a repertoire of goal-directed behaviors for use during adversity
- Recognize and challenge narratives of helplessness or powerlessness
- Experience and celebrate small wins as evidence of agency

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Behavioral activation
- Goal-setting with successive approximation (shaping)
- Self-monitoring and self-reinforcement
- Response generalization practice

**ACT-Based:**
- Values clarification exercises
- Committed action planning
- Defusion from helplessness narratives
- Present-moment engagement with agency opportunities

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Agentic resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Agentic-Generative subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Frequency of goal-directed behaviors outside sessions
- Self-efficacy ratings (self-report scales)
- Goal attainment (percentage of planned actions completed)
