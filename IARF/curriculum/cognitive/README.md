# Cognitive-Narrative Dimension Curriculum

> *The stories we tell about our suffering shape what that suffering becomes. A new story is not a lie — it is an act of resilience.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Cognitive-Narrative** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to construct adaptive meanings, maintain cognitive flexibility, and tell empowering stories about one's experience — especially through and after adversity.

This dimension spans cognitive science (how we think) and narrative identity (the story we tell about who we are). Both are powerful levers for resilience.

People with strong cognitive-narrative resilience:
- Can consider multiple perspectives on a situation
- Do not catastrophize or overgeneralize from adversity
- Tell stories about their adversity that include growth, learning, or strength
- Demonstrate cognitive flexibility under pressure
- Hold a coherent, forward-oriented narrative of their identity

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Identify unhelpful thinking patterns and their effects
- Practice cognitive flexibility and perspective-taking
- Construct or revise their personal narrative of adversity in growth-oriented ways
- Use defusion techniques to unhook from unhelpful thoughts
- Apply adaptive attribution patterns to difficult events

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Cognitive flexibility training (discrimination training for perspective-taking)
- Attribution retraining
- Behavior chains for adaptive meaning-making
- Reinforcement of flexible, growth-oriented cognition

**ACT-Based:**
- Cognitive defusion techniques (leaves on a stream, naming the story, thanking the mind)
- Self-as-context exercises — observer perspective
- Acceptance of the full complexity of one's story
- Values-grounded narrative reconstruction

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Cognitive-narrative resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Cognitive-Narrative subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Cognitive flexibility ratings (in-session perspective-taking tasks)
- Defusion skill use frequency (self-report)
- Narrative coherence and growth-orientation (qualitative assessment)
- Attribution pattern tracking
