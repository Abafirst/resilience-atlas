# Emotional-Adaptive Dimension Curriculum

> *Resilience is not the absence of emotion — it is the capacity to feel everything and still move forward.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Emotional-Adaptive** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to experience, process, and respond adaptively to the full range of emotional experience — especially during and after adversity.

People with strong emotional resilience:
- Can identify and name what they are feeling
- Allow themselves to feel difficult emotions without shutting them down
- Can shift their emotional state when the situation calls for it
- Express emotions in ways that serve their relationships and goals
- Do not get stuck in emotional states

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Identify and label a wide range of emotional states
- Practice acceptance of difficult emotions (rather than avoidance)
- Use emotional regulation strategies effectively
- Express emotions in context-appropriate ways
- Recognize patterns in their emotional responses to adversity

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Emotion identification and labeling (discrimination training)
- Reinforcement of emotional expression
- Behavior chains for self-regulation strategies
- Exposure-based work for emotional avoidance

**ACT-Based:**
- Acceptance exercises — willingness to have difficult emotions
- Defusion from emotional narratives ("I am broken", "I can't handle this")
- Present-moment awareness of emotional experience
- Values connection to emotional processing goals

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Emotional resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Emotional-Adaptive subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Emotion identification accuracy (in-session ratings)
- Experiential avoidance measures
- Emotional regulation skill use (self-report)
- Emotional expression quality in role-play or reported interactions
