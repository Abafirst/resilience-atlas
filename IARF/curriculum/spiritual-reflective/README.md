# Spiritual-Reflective Dimension Curriculum

> *Meaning is not found — it is made. And the making of meaning is itself a resilient act.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Spiritual-Reflective** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to find or construct meaning, purpose, and existential grounding — through personal values, beliefs, community, or connection to something larger than oneself.

This dimension does not require religious belief. It includes secular meaning-making, philosophical grounding, and any practice that connects a person to purpose beyond immediate circumstance.

People with strong spiritual-reflective resilience:
- Know their values and live in alignment with them
- Find meaning even in painful experiences
- Maintain a sense of purpose through adversity
- Have regular reflective practices that sustain their inner life
- Feel connected to something larger than themselves

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Articulate their core personal values
- Practice finding or constructing meaning in difficult experiences
- Establish regular reflective habits (journaling, prayer, meditation, community)
- Connect daily actions and goals to deeper purpose
- Engage with existential questions without avoidance

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Behavioral habit-building for reflective practices
- Values-aligned goal setting with reinforcement
- Behavior chains for meaning-making activities

**ACT-Based:**
- Values clarification (ACT's primary values exercises)
- Committed action planning
- Self-as-context — observer perspective on adversity
- Meaning-making through defusion from catastrophic narratives

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Spiritual-reflective resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Spiritual-Reflective subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Frequency of reflective practice behaviors
- Values clarity ratings (self-report)
- Meaning-finding in recent adversity (narrative assessment)
- Alignment between stated values and recent actions
