# Relational-Connective Dimension Curriculum

> *We are wired for connection — and resilience flourishes in relationship.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Relational-Connective** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to access, cultivate, and maintain meaningful social connection as a resource during adversity.

People with strong relational resilience:
- Reach out for support when they need it
- Maintain relationships under pressure
- Can accept help without shame
- Offer support to others reciprocally
- Have a network of meaningful connections to draw on

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Identify existing social supports and how to access them
- Practice asking for and receiving help
- Build communication skills that strengthen relationships
- Develop new connections where networks are thin
- Navigate conflict and repair in important relationships

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Social skills training (modeling, rehearsal, feedback)
- Communication skills building
- Relationship behavior chains
- Positive reinforcement for connection-seeking behaviors

**ACT-Based:**
- Acceptance of vulnerability
- Present-moment engagement in relationship
- Defusion from "burden" and "weakness" narratives
- Values-based relationship goals

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Relational resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Relational-Connective subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Frequency of support-seeking behaviors
- Quality of communication (self-report, role-play assessment)
- Relationship satisfaction ratings
- Network size and diversity tracking
