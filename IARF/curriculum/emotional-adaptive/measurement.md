# Emotional-Adaptive Dimension: Measurement & Monitoring

---

## Overview

Emotional measurement in the IARF requires multi-modal assessment: behavioral data (frequency of emotional skills use, coping behavior, adaptive expression), self-report ratings (emotion regulation quality, subjective distress), standardized instruments, and clinician observation. This document provides a comprehensive measurement framework for the Emotional-Adaptive dimension.

Measurement in this dimension serves both clinical decision-making (what does this client need, and is the intervention working?) and client empowerment (helping clients see their own progress in concrete, meaningful terms).

---

## Measurement Philosophy

Emotional measurement in the IARF is:
- **Multi-modal**: No single measure captures the complexity of emotional functioning
- **Strengths-visible**: Measures are designed to capture adaptive functioning, not only deficit
- **Trauma-informed**: Data collection respects the vulnerability of emotional disclosure
- **Process-focused**: Measures capture emotional skill-building processes, not only outcomes
- **Client-meaningful**: Data is shared and interpreted collaboratively with clients

## Emotion Regulation Success Rate Measures

### Weekly Emotion Regulation Tracking

The Emotion Regulation Success Rate (ERSR) measures the percentage of emotional challenge events in which the client uses an adaptive coping response (vs. maladaptive response or no response).

**Procedure**:
1. Client logs all significant emotional events during the week (minimum: those rated ≥5/10 on distress scale)
2. For each event, records: emotion, intensity (0–10), coping response used, outcome (adaptive/neutral/maladaptive)
3. ERSR = (Adaptive responses ÷ Total emotional events) × 100

## Emotional Skill Acquisition Data

| Target Skill | Measurement Method | Mastery Criterion |
|---|---|---|
| Emotion Identification (vocabulary) | In-session labeling accuracy | Correctly labels ≥10 distinct emotions with 90% accuracy; 3 sessions |
| Body-Emotion Connection | In-session description quality | Accurately locates and describes somatic correlate of emotion; 4 sessions |
| Coping Strategy Use | Event recording + client report | Uses adaptive coping in ≥80% of high-distress events; 4 weeks |
| FCT Replacement Use | Event recording | Uses replacement communication instead of challenging behavior in ≥75% of trigger situations; 4 weeks |
| Distress Tolerance | Duration of non-crisis persistence | Tolerates distress for ≥20 minutes without maladaptive response; 3 consecutive events |
| Adaptive Emotional Expression | In-session observation + self-report | Expresses emotion in non-blaming, specific terms across 3 real situations |
| Post-Emotional Recovery | Duration recording | Returns to functional baseline within 60 minutes after moderate emotional event; 6 consecutive events |

## Progress Indicators

### Short-Term (4–8 Weeks)
- DERS total score decreases ≥10 points from baseline
- DASS-21 subscale scores show clinically meaningful improvement (≥5 points on relevant subscale)
- Client can name ≥8 distinct emotions accurately
- Uses at least 1 adaptive coping strategy in 50% of high-distress events
- Reports use of FCT replacement in at least 2 real-world situations
- ERSR reaches ≥50% within first 8 weeks

### Medium-Term (3–6 Months)
- DERS total score in non-clinical range or ≥30% reduction from baseline
- Post-emotional recovery time reduced from baseline measure
- ERSR ≥70% sustained across 4 consecutive weeks
- Demonstrates emotion regulation strategies across at least 3 different emotional types
- Reports and demonstrates values-informed response to at least 2 significant emotional challenges
- Body-emotion connection established (can locate and describe emotions in body)

### Long-Term (6–12 Months)
- ERSR ≥80% maintained at 12-month review
- DERS in normative range
- Post-traumatic emotional growth demonstrated (PTGI or clinical observation)
- Client uses emotion vocabulary and self-compassion language spontaneously and naturally
- Demonstrates emotional support to others (not only recipient of support)
- Reports emotional growth as a meaningful aspect of their resilience narrative

## Clinical Decision Points

| Indicator | Clinical Action |
|---|---|
| DERS total ≥100 at any assessment | Review for complex trauma; consider referral for specialized trauma therapy |
| DASS-21 depression subscale ≥14 (severe) | Screen for depression severity; consider medication referral; increase support |
| ERSR <30% after 8 weeks of intervention | Conduct functional reassessment; revise protocol; consider adding DBT-informed skills [Research citation needed] |
| Increases in self-injurious behavior | Immediate safety assessment; revise crisis prevention plan; consider step-up in care |
| Zero emotional disclosure across 3 consecutive sessions | Assess for avoidance, dissociation, or trauma activation; shift to somatic stabilization |
| Rapid, unexplained emotional instability | Consider biological factors; consult for psychiatric evaluation |
