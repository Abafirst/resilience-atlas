# Cognitive-Narrative Dimension: Measurement & Monitoring

---

## Overview

Measurement in the Cognitive-Narrative dimension captures the evolution of thinking patterns, narrative structure, cognitive flexibility, and adaptive interpretation over the course of treatment. This dimension presents particular measurement challenges because cognition and narrative are internal processes, but it also offers rich behavioral indicators — thought records, written narratives, verbal productions in session, and standardized self-report — that together provide a comprehensive clinical picture.

This document provides a multi-modal measurement framework for the Cognitive-Narrative dimension within The Resilience Atlas™ IATLAS.

---

## Measurement Philosophy

Cognitive-narrative measurement in the IATLAS is:
- **Process-focused**: Measuring how clients are thinking, not only what they conclude
- **Narrative-sensitive**: Recognizing that some of the most important changes appear in narrative quality and richness
- **Non-pathologizing**: Measuring adaptive thinking gains, not only cognitive deficit reduction
- **Collaborative**: Clients participate in rating their own thinking and narrative evolution
- **Trauma-aware**: Fragmented or incoherent narratives are understood within their trauma context

## Narrative Flexibility Ratings

Narrative flexibility is clinician-rated based on session observation. It reflects the degree to which the client's narrative about themselves and their life allows for complexity, growth, and revision.

### Narrative Flexibility Rating Scale (Clinician-Rated)

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Narrative is rigid, single-perspective, and cannot accommodate alternative interpretations |
| 2 | Narrative shows slight flexibility when directly prompted but returns to rigid baseline |
| 3 | Narrative shows moderate flexibility; client can consider alternatives but defaults to dominant narrative |
| 4 | Narrative is notably flexible; client spontaneously offers alternative perspectives; narrative includes complexity |
| 5 | Narrative is highly flexible, complex, and coherent; includes adversity and growth; client relates to narrative as author, not character |

Rate at baseline and every 4–6 sessions. Document specific evidence for the rating in progress notes.

## Progress Indicators

### Short-Term (4–8 Weeks)

- Client completes thought records ≥3 times/week for 4 consecutive weeks
- Demonstrates basic reframing with clinician prompting across 3+ sessions
- Can identify at least 2 personal cognitive patterns by name
- Growth mindset language appears in session at least once per session without prompting
- Narrative Flexibility Rating increases ≥1 level from baseline
- Reports using at least one defusion technique in real life

### Medium-Term (3–6 Months)

- Spontaneous reframing (≥2 per session) without prompting for 4 consecutive sessions
- Thought record quality rating ≥3 consistently
- Perspective-taking exercise: generates ≥3 perspectives without significant difficulty
- Cognitive Flexibility Scale score increases ≥5 points from baseline
- Narrative Flexibility Rating ≥3
- RRS brooding subscale shows clinically meaningful decrease
- Client spontaneously uses narrative language: "the story my mind is telling" or "the author perspective"

### Long-Term (6–12 Months)

- Narrative Flexibility Rating ≥4
- PTGI shows clinically meaningful change (if applicable)
- Reframing occurs naturally in session without any prompting
- Client uses defusion language and techniques with others (as mentor or model)
- Life narrative coherently integrates adversity and growth
- CFS in normative range
- Client articulates a clear, values-anchored life narrative without prompting

## Clinical Decision Points

| Indicator | Clinical Action |
|---|---|
| Thought records consistently incomplete after 6 weeks | Assess barrier; simplify form; address avoidance; consider alternative format |
| Narrative Flexibility Rating unchanged at 1 after 3 months | Reassess for trauma-related narrative disruption; consider trauma processing approach; adjust narrative goals |
| RRS brooding subscale increases from baseline | Assess for depression severity; increase behavioral activation; add explicit rumination interruption protocol |
| Client unable to take any alternative perspective after extended practice | Consider neuropsychological assessment; explore rigid thinking as presenting feature; consult |
| PTGI scores decrease despite apparent progress | Explore whether externally-imposed growth framing is creating shame; revisit pacing of narrative integration work |
| Growth mindset language appears but behavior remains fixed | Assess gap between cognitive change and behavioral change; increase behavioral experiment component |
