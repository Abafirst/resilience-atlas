# Agentic-Generative Dimension: Measurement & Monitoring

---

## Overview

Measurement in the Agentic-Generative dimension is guided by two core commitments: data-driven precision and values-aligned meaningfulness. The IATLAS recognizes that measurement must serve the client's growth, not merely document it. Every data point collected should be interpretable, actionable, and connected to something the client cares about.

This document provides a comprehensive measurement system for the Agentic-Generative dimension, including behavioral frequency measures, skill acquisition data, standardized assessment tools, self-report scales, and progress monitoring templates. Taken together, these tools provide a complete picture of a client's growth across the domains of agency, initiative, goal pursuit, self-efficacy, and purposeful action.

---

## Measurement Philosophy

**Data-driven**: Decisions about what to target, when to progress, and when to revise are based on observable, recorded data — not impressions or assumptions.

**Values-aligned**: Measurement targets should include domains the client finds meaningful, not merely domains that are easy to operationalize.

**Collaborative**: Clients are partners in measurement. They understand what is being tracked and why, and they participate in interpreting results.

**Sensitive to change**: Measures should be sensitive enough to detect the small, incremental changes that characterize early skill development.

**Trauma-informed**: Data collection processes should not be re-traumatizing. Self-report items are carefully worded. Performance data is interpreted with compassion, not criticism.

## Skill Acquisition Data

The following table summarizes measurement methods for key Agentic-Generative skills. "Mastery criterion" refers to the performance threshold at which a skill is considered established and the next target can be introduced.

| Target Skill | Measurement Method | Frequency | Mastery Criterion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Values Identification | Clinician observation + written exercise completion | Per session (early phase) | Can name 3+ values with behavioral specificity; 2 consecutive sessions |
| Basic Goal Setting | Task analysis checklist | Per session | Completes all 10 goal-setting steps independently; 3 consecutive sessions |
| Action Initiation | Latency from task assignment to initiation | Per session | Initiates within 5 minutes of assignment, without prompting; 4 consecutive sessions |

| Values-Action Alignment | Values Thermometer score | Monthly | Average alignment ≥7 across top 3 values; 2 consecutive months |

## Progress Indicators

### Short-Term Indicators (4–8 Weeks)

- Completes Activity Log for at least 5 of 7 days per week
- Can name 3+ personal values without prompting
- Demonstrates Basic Goal Setting task analysis ≥70% independently
- Completes ≥50% of scheduled activation activities
- Identifies at least one barrier before it derails a goal
- Shows measurable increase in activity log accomplishment ratings compared to baseline
- Reports reduced frequency of avoidance behaviors
- Generates at least one efficacy statement per session unprompted

### Medium-Term Indicators (3–6 Months)

- Values-Action Alignment (Thermometer) score ≥6 for top 3 values
- Completes 3+ multi-step goal plans with minimal clinician support
- Demonstrates flexible problem solving across novel problems (3+ solutions per challenge)
- Maintains goal pursuit across at least one significant setback without abandoning the goal
- Reports initiation of at least 2 new valued activities that were previously avoided
- Self-monitoring data shows ≥80% accuracy
- Initiative Under Uncertainty demonstrated across at least 2 novel situations
- AAQ-II score shows clinically meaningful reduction in experiential avoidance

### Long-Term Indicators (6–12 Months)

- Resilient Agency: demonstrates consistent values-aligned action across 6+ months including periods of stress
- Can independently design and implement a behavior change plan without clinician support
- Demonstrates Long-Range Vision Planning with a 1–3 year values-anchored life plan
- Begins mentoring or supporting others in goal-setting or resilience skills
- Self-Efficacy Scale scores move into normative range
- Sustained reduction in avoidance measured at 12-month follow-up
- Reports subjective sense of purposeful living (rated ≥7 on 10-point scale) across multiple assessments

## Standardized Assessment Tools

The following standardized tools are recommended for use in the Agentic-Generative dimension:

| Tool | What It Measures | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Valued Living Questionnaire (VLQ) | Importance and consistency of valued living across domains | Baseline; every 12 weeks | Free; validated; 10 domains [Research citation needed] |
| Action and Acceptance Questionnaire–II (AAQ-II) | Psychological flexibility / experiential avoidance | Baseline; every 8 weeks | 7-item; well-validated for ACT outcomes [Research citation needed] |

## Self-Report Measures

### IATLAS Agentic-Generative Weekly Self-Rating Scale

*Instructions: Rate each item from 0 (not at all true this week) to 10 (completely true this week).*

| Item | Rating (0–10) |
|---|---|
| 1. This week, I took action on something that matters to me. | |
| 2. I set at least one clear goal this week. | |
| 3. When I hit an obstacle, I kept going (or problem-solved a way forward). | |
| 4. I did something meaningful even when I didn't feel like it. | |
| 5. I asked for help when I needed it. | |
| 6. My behavior this week reflected what I genuinely value. | |
| 7. I felt capable of handling challenges that came up. | |
| 8. I made at least one decision based on my values, not just what felt easy. | |
| 9. I completed what I planned to complete this week. | |
| 10. I feel like I'm moving in a direction that matters to me. | |

**Scoring**: Sum of all items (max 100). Scores below 40 indicate significant agentic deficits warranting immediate clinical review. Track weekly change over time.

## Goal Attainment Scaling (GAS)

Goal Attainment Scaling is a personalized outcome measurement approach that allows clients and clinicians to define expected levels of performance for a specific goal and track progress relative to those expectations.

### How to Use GAS in the Agentic-Generative Dimension

**Step 1**: Collaboratively identify 2–3 specific agentic goals for the treatment period

**Step 2**: For each goal, define five levels of attainment:

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| -2 | Much less than expected | Outcome significantly below what was hoped for |
| -1 | Somewhat less than expected | Partial progress; below target |
| 0 | Expected outcome | Target achieved as planned |
| +1 | Somewhat better than expected | Exceeded the original target |
| +2 | Much better than expected | Outcome significantly exceeded expectations |

**Step 3**: Rate progress at regular intervals (every 4–6 weeks)

**Step 4**: Calculate GAS T-score using standard formula (mean score of 50 = expected outcomes achieved) [Research citation needed]

### Example GAS Goal: Behavioral Activation

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| -2 | Completes fewer than 2 scheduled activities per week for 4 consecutive weeks |
| -1 | Completes 2–3 scheduled activities per week; below the 4-activity target |
| 0 | Completes 4+ scheduled activities per week for 4 consecutive weeks |
| +1 | Completes 4+ activities AND spontaneously adds self-initiated activities |
| +2 | Completes 6+ activities per week including 2+ new values-based activities; self-reports high sense of purpose |

## Data Interpretation Guidelines

- **Rising trend**: 3+ consecutive weeks of improvement → skill is being acquired; maintain current approach
- **Plateau**: 3+ consecutive weeks without change → reassess; adjust reinforcement, prompt level, or target
- **Regression**: Clinically meaningful decrease → assess for changes in life circumstances, trauma activation, or skill generalization issues
- **High variability**: Week-to-week instability → identify antecedent conditions that produce better vs. worse performance; address variability before progressing to higher-level skills

## Red Flags and Clinical Decision Points

The following indicators should prompt immediate clinical review and possible protocol modification:

- ☐ Client reports complete inability to initiate any action for 2+ consecutive weeks → Screen for depression severity; consider referral for medication evaluation; reduce demands; increase support
- ☐ Avoidance behaviors increase significantly from baseline despite intervention → Reassess function of avoidance; consider trauma activation; consult supervisor
- ☐ Client expresses hopelessness about capacity to change despite early gains → Prioritize ACT defusion and acceptance work; assess for hopelessness as clinical risk factor
- ☐ Goal Attainment Scaling scores remain at -2 across 3+ assessment points → Fundamentally reassess targets; ensure goals are values-based, not clinician-imposed; consider different approach
- ☐ Self-Efficacy Scale scores decrease over time despite intervention → Assess for trauma reactivation, environmental stressors, or mismatch between skill targets and current capacity
- ☐ Client reports significant dissociation when attempting action initiation → Shift to somatic-regulative stabilization work before continuing agentic skill-building
