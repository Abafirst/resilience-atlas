# Somatic-Regulative Dimension Curriculum

> *The body keeps the score — and it also holds the key. Resilience lives in the nervous system.*

---

## Dimension Overview

The **Somatic-Regulative** dimension of resilience describes the capacity to maintain physical regulation, body awareness, and somatic equilibrium — especially during stress and adversity.

This dimension recognizes that resilience is not just cognitive or emotional — it is **embodied**. The body's capacity to return to regulation after activation is a core resilience competency.

People with strong somatic resilience:
- Notice physical signs of stress early (before they become overwhelming)
- Have a repertoire of effective body-based regulation strategies
- Maintain basic physical self-care under pressure
- Are not dissociated from or frightened by their own physical sensations
- Use their body as a resource rather than experiencing it as a threat

---

## Curriculum Goals

By the end of this curriculum, clients will be able to:
- Identify physical signs of stress and activation in their own body
- Use at least three somatic regulation strategies effectively
- Maintain basic physical self-care practices during adversity
- Approach bodily sensations with curiosity rather than fear
- Build body-based resilience practices as daily habits

## Key Interventions

**ABA-Based:**
- Interoception training (body awareness skill-building)
- Self-regulation behavioral chains (notice → breathe → ground → re-engage)
- Physical self-care habit-building with reinforcement
- Data collection on somatic practice frequency

**ACT-Based:**
- Present-moment awareness through the body
- Acceptance of difficult physical sensations
- Defusion from "danger" narratives about bodily experience
- Values-connected somatic practice goals

---

## Trauma Considerations

The somatic dimension is often where trauma is most directly present. Important guidelines:
- **Always follow the client's lead** on pacing and depth of somatic work
- **Avoid overwhelm** — grounding before and after somatic awareness exercises
- **Normalize dissociation** as a historical protective response, not a failure
- **Build safety first** before deepening interoceptive work
- **Consult trauma specialists** when somatic trauma presentations are complex

---

## Assessment & Monitoring

Somatic resilience is measured by the Resilience Atlas™ (Somatic-Regulative subscale).

Session-level monitoring targets:
- Frequency of somatic practice behaviors (outside sessions)
- Regulation strategy use during stress (self-report)
- Interoceptive awareness accuracy (body scan exercises)
- Physical self-care consistency
