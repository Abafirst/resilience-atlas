# Somatic-Regulative Dimension: Measurement & Monitoring

---

## Overview

Somatic measurement in the IATLAS captures the behavioral, physiological, and subjective dimensions of physical wellbeing and somatic regulation. This dimension combines objective behavioral measures (frequency of somatic practices, sleep duration, physical activity), physiological self-report (energy levels, pain, stress response indicators), and subjective ratings of body awareness and somatic safety.

The goal of somatic measurement is to provide a data-rich picture of the client's physical resilience that supports clinical decision-making and demonstrates measurable growth over time.

## Somatic Awareness Ratings

Somatic awareness is measured using the IATLAS Somatic Awareness Scale (SAS):

*Rate from 0 (not at all true) to 10 (completely true):*

| Item | Rating (0–10) |
|---|---|
| 1. I can notice physical sensations in my body without becoming distressed. | |
| 2. I am aware of when my body is tense or relaxed. | |
| 3. I can locate where in my body I feel stress or emotion. | |
| 4. I respond to my body's signals (fatigue, hunger, pain) before they become urgent. | |
| 5. I engage in at least one regular somatic practice (breathing, movement, relaxation). | |
| 6. My body feels more like an ally than an enemy most of the time. | |
| 7. I can use my breath to shift my physical state when needed. | |
| 8. I recover from physical stress in a reasonable amount of time. | |
| 9. I take care of my body's basic needs (sleep, nutrition, hydration) most days. | |
| 10. I have a sense of physical safety in my body most of the time. | |

**Scoring**: Total out of 100. Scores below 40 indicate significant somatic wellbeing deficits.

## Progress Indicators

### Short-Term (4–8 Weeks)
- Establishes at least one regular somatic practice (≥3x/week for 4 consecutive weeks)
- Sleep duration increases toward ≥7 hours in clients with sleep deficit
- Physical activity increases from baseline by at least 30 minutes/week
- Somatic Awareness Scale (SAS) total increases ≥5 points
- PSS-10 score shows clinically meaningful decrease
- Client reports at least one effective use of somatic regulation during a stressful event

### Medium-Term (3–6 Months)
- PMR or equivalent practice established as a habit (self-sustaining, without clinical prompting)
- PSQI score in good sleep range (≤5) or significant improvement from baseline
- Physical activity meets minimum health guidelines (150 min moderate/week) OR meaningful improvement from baseline if unable to meet guidelines
- SAS total ≥60 (indicating reasonable somatic awareness and safety)
- Body relationship has shifted from predominantly adversarial to at least neutral; client can articulate body's capacities with some appreciation
- Grounding techniques used effectively during at least 2 tracked stressful events

### Long-Term (6–12 Months)
- Sustained somatic practice across 6+ months including periods of life stress
- Sleep, nutrition, and hydration self-care behaviors maintained without clinical prompting
- Physical activity is a valued, self-directed part of the client's life
- Body is experienced as an ally or resource (SAS Item 6 ≥7)
- Client demonstrates ability to self-regulate physically under moderate stress
- Somatic practices generalized to all major life contexts

## Clinical Decision Points

| Indicator | Clinical Action |
|---|---|
| Sleep duration consistently <5 hours | Implement CBT-I protocol; consider sleep hygiene intensive; refer for medical evaluation |
| Physical activity at zero despite 8 weeks of intervention | Reassess barriers; consider graduated shaping; address medical or mobility issues |
| PHQ-15 score ≥15 (severe somatic symptoms) | Consider referral for medical evaluation; may indicate untreated physical condition |
| SAS total <30 after 12 weeks | Reassess for trauma-related somatic dissociation; shift to trauma-specialized somatic intervention |
| Client reports severe aversion to body-based practices | Do not push; assess for body-focused trauma; begin with external grounding only |
| Significant unexplained pain increase | Refer for medical evaluation before continuing somatic practices |
