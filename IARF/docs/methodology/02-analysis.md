# Phase 2: ABA-Informed Analysis

> *Data without interpretation is noise. Analysis transforms assessment into direction.*

---

## Overview

The Analysis Phase transforms raw assessment data into a clinically meaningful picture of how resilience works — and where it breaks down — for a specific individual. Drawing on ABA's tradition of functional analysis and behavior-analytic thinking, this phase identifies patterns, hypothesizes functions, and prioritizes targets for intervention.

**Goals of Phase 2:**
- Interpret dimensional assessment data within context
- Identify functional relationships between behaviors and environment
- Develop hypotheses about what maintains current resilience patterns
- Prioritize dimensions and targets for intervention
- Create a coherent, individualized case conceptualization

---

## The Functional Analysis Framework

IATLAS adapts ABA's **ABC (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence)** model to resilience analysis:

### Antecedents
Conditions that set the occasion for resilient or non-resilient responding:
- Environmental stressors (work, family, community)
- Internal states (emotional, somatic, cognitive)
- Relational dynamics (support availability, conflict patterns)
- Historical triggers (trauma reminders, past adversity patterns)

### Behaviors
Observable and measurable expressions of each resilience dimension:
- What does the person *do* when encountering adversity?
- Which adaptive behaviors are present? Which are absent or inconsistent?
- What behaviors reflect each of the six dimensions?

### Consequences
What follows resilient or non-resilient behavior — and what maintains current patterns:
- What reinforces adaptive resilience responses?
- What reinforces avoidance, rigidity, or withdrawal?
- What natural consequences support or undermine resilience-building?

---

## Dimensional Pattern Analysis

For each of the six dimensions, analyze:

| Analysis Step | Questions to Answer |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Baseline Review** | What does the score tell us? Where does this person fall relative to the full range? |
| **Strength Mapping** | What specific capacities within this dimension are already active? |
| **Gap Analysis** | What skills, knowledge, or opportunities are missing? |
| **Functional Hypothesis** | What function do current patterns serve? What would change if this dimension grew? |
| **Interaction Effects** | How does this dimension interact with others? What cascades might occur? |

---

## Case Conceptualization

Bring the dimensional analyses together into a unified **Case Conceptualization Summary**:

1. **Individual Profile Narrative** — A qualitative description of the person's overall resilience landscape
2. **Dimensional Priority Ranking** — Which 1–3 dimensions represent the highest-leverage intervention targets?
3. **Functional Hypotheses** — Best-supported explanations for current patterns
4. **Strengths to Leverage** — Specific existing capacities that can anchor the intervention
5. **Contextual Considerations** — Environmental, cultural, and relational factors to integrate
6. **Contraindications** — Any factors that suggest caution or modified approach

---

## ACT-Informed Analysis

Alongside the ABA functional lens, analysis also considers ACT's **Hexaflex** of psychological flexibility:

- **Acceptance** vs. experiential avoidance — Is the person fighting their internal experience?
- **Defusion** vs. cognitive fusion — Are they entangled with unhelpful thoughts?
- **Present moment** vs. rigid time perspective — Are they stuck in past or future?
- **Self-as-context** vs. self-as-content — Is identity fused with adversity narratives?
- **Values** — Are values clear and guiding action?
- **Committed action** — Is behavior aligned with values under pressure?

---

## Output: Analysis Report

At the close of Phase 2, produce:

1. **Functional Analysis Summary** — ABC analysis per dimension
2. **Case Conceptualization** — Integrated narrative and hypothesis
3. **Priority Targets** — Ranked intervention targets with rationale
4. **Provisional Intervention Direction** — Initial thinking on approaches and strategies
5. **Questions for Phase 3** — Open questions to explore during intervention design

This output feeds directly into Phase 3: Intervention.
