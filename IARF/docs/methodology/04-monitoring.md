# Phase 4: Progress Monitoring & Outcome Measurement

> *In ABA, data is not paperwork — it is the conversation between the clinician and the behavior. It tells us what's working, what isn't, and what to do next.*

---

## Overview

The Monitoring Phase is not a final step — it is a **continuous process** that runs in parallel with intervention. IATLAS's monitoring system tracks progress at multiple levels: session by session, dimension by dimension, and across the full intervention arc. Data drives every decision.

**Goals of Phase 4:**
- Collect meaningful, actionable data on intervention targets
- Track progress toward short-term objectives and long-term goals
- Identify when interventions are working and when they need modification
- Measure overall resilience growth across the six dimensions
- Document outcomes for accountability, learning, and research

---

## Levels of Monitoring

### Level 1: Session-Level Data
Collected at every contact point:
- Skill performance data (frequency, duration, rate, percentage correct, etc.)
- Qualitative session notes
- Client-reported experience and feedback
- Fidelity checks on intervention procedures

→ Use the [Progress Note Template](../../templates/progress-note.md)

### Level 2: Objective-Level Progress
Reviewed weekly or bi-weekly:
- Progress toward each Short-Term Objective (STO)
- Graph or visual display of data trends
- Decision rules applied: continue, modify, or move on?

### Level 3: Dimensional Progress
Reviewed monthly:
- Progress across each targeted resilience dimension
- Comparison to baseline dimensional scores
- Shifts in functioning not captured by specific objectives

### Level 4: Outcome Measurement
At plan review or program completion:
- Re-administration of the Resilience Atlas™ assessment
- Comparison of pre/post dimensional profiles
- Goal attainment scaling
- Client narrative of perceived change

---

## Data Collection Methods

| Method | Best For | Frequency |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Frequency/Event Recording** | Discrete behavioral skills | Per session |
| **Duration Recording** | Sustained behaviors (e.g., mindfulness practice) | Per session |
| **Interval Recording** | Behaviors that are difficult to count individually | Per session |
| **Rating Scales** | Self-reported emotional states, skill confidence | Per session |
| **Standardized Assessment** | Dimensional resilience profiles | Pre/post and at key intervals |
| **Goal Attainment Scaling** | Individualized meaningful outcomes | At plan review |
| **Qualitative Narrative** | Meaning, experience, and context | Per session |

---

## Visual Analysis & Decision-Making

IATLAS uses ABA's tradition of **visual analysis** of graphed data:

### When to Continue the Current Plan
- Data trend shows consistent progress toward objectives
- Variability is acceptable and within expected range
- Client engagement and motivation are maintained

### When to Modify the Plan
- Data shows flat trend (no progress) for 3–5 consecutive sessions
- Data shows reversal (regression) without clear explanation
- A new barrier or contextual factor has emerged
- Client reports the intervention is not resonating

### When to Advance or Close a Goal
- Criterion for mastery has been met across sessions and settings
- Generalization of the skill to natural environment is demonstrated
- New, higher-level objectives are appropriate

---

## Generalization Monitoring

A skill is not "learned" until it is generalized. Monitor:

- **Setting generalization** — Does the skill appear outside sessions?
- **Person generalization** — Does the skill work with people other than the clinician?
- **Stimulus generalization** — Does the skill apply across varied situations?
- **Maintenance** — Does the skill persist over time without direct support?

---

## Outcome Reporting

At program conclusion, compile an **Outcome Summary Report** including:

1. **Pre/Post Resilience Profile** — Dimensional scores at intake vs. completion
2. **Goal Achievement Summary** — LTGs and STOs: met, partially met, not met
3. **Behavioral Change Evidence** — Data graphs and trend summaries
4. **Client Narrative** — Client's own account of change (qualitative)
5. **Clinician Observations** — Functional changes observed across settings
6. **Recommendations** — Next steps, ongoing supports, or continued intervention areas

## The Continuous IATLAS Cycle

Monitoring feeds back into all preceding phases:

```
Assessment → Analysis → Intervention → Monitoring
     ↑                                     |
     └─────────── Feedback Loop ───────────┘
```

New data from monitoring may prompt:
- Return to **Assessment** (re-administer instruments)
- Return to **Analysis** (revise functional hypotheses)
- Modification of **Intervention** (change strategies or targets)

This iterative cycle is what makes IATLAS a living, adaptive framework — not a static protocol.
