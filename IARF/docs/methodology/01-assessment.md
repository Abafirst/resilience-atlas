# Phase 1: Comprehensive Resilience Assessment

> *Assessment is not just measurement — it is the beginning of the therapeutic alliance and the foundation for everything that follows.*

---

## Overview

The Assessment Phase establishes a complete, multi-dimensional picture of an individual's current resilience profile. Using the Resilience Atlas™ as the primary instrument, supplemented by clinical observation and structured interviews, this phase generates the data needed to guide all subsequent IARF work.

**Goals of Phase 1:**
- Establish baseline resilience scores across all six dimensions
- Identify dimensional strengths and areas of growth
- Understand contextual factors influencing resilience expression
- Build rapport and shared understanding with the client
- Create a collaborative foundation for the intervention plan

---

## Assessment Instruments

### Primary: Resilience Atlas™ Assessment
The 72-question Resilience Atlas™ assessment provides scored profiles across six dimensions:
- Agentic-Generative
- Relational-Connective
- Spiritual-Reflective
- Emotional-Adaptive
- Somatic-Regulative
- Cognitive-Narrative

**Administration:**
- Administer before any intervention begins
- Allow 20–30 minutes for completion
- Provide a neutral, comfortable environment
- Avoid prompting or influencing responses

## Assessment Domains

For each of the six dimensions, the assessment examines:

| Domain | What We're Measuring |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Strengths** | Existing resilience capacities that can be leveraged |
| **Barriers** | Behavioral, environmental, or cognitive obstacles |
| **History** | Past experiences with adversity and recovery |
| **Context** | Current stressors, supports, and environmental factors |
| **Motivation** | Readiness for change and personal goals |

---

## ABA-Informed Assessment Considerations

Drawing from Applied Behavior Analysis, the IARF assessment includes a **functional lens**:

- **Antecedents**: What conditions precede resilient or non-resilient responses?
- **Behaviors**: What observable behaviors reflect each resilience dimension?
- **Consequences**: What reinforces existing patterns, adaptive or maladaptive?

This functional perspective is developed further in Phase 2 (Analysis).

---

## Trauma-Informed Approach

All assessment activities follow trauma-informed principles:
- **Safety first** — ensure physical and emotional safety before, during, and after assessment
- **Transparency** — explain the purpose and process clearly
- **Choice** — offer options wherever possible (setting, pacing, format)
- **Collaboration** — frame assessment as a partnership, not an evaluation
- **Empowerment** — emphasize strengths alongside growth areas

---

## Output: Assessment Summary

At the close of Phase 1, compile:

1. **Dimensional Scores** — numerical profile from the Resilience Atlas™
2. **Qualitative Notes** — observations from the intake interview and clinical observation
3. **Strengths Inventory** — explicit list of identified resilience strengths
4. **Priority Dimensions** — 1–2 dimensions flagged for initial intervention focus
5. **Contextual Factors Summary** — relevant life history, stressors, and supports

This output feeds directly into Phase 2: Analysis.
