About the Resilience Atlas

Built on Research. Grounded in Purpose.

The Resilience Atlas grew from doctoral research into how people adapt, recover, and grow under pressure — and a belief that resilience can be mapped, understood, and cultivated.

J

Janeen Molchany

Ph.D. BCBA Researcher Author

Janeen Molchany is a doctoral-level researcher, Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA), and the creator of the Resilience Atlas framework. Her work bridges positive psychology, behavioral science, and resilience research to create practical tools for understanding and building human resilience.

The Founder Story

The Resilience Atlas began as a doctoral research project exploring how individuals navigate adversity across multiple interacting dimensions. What started as an academic inquiry became a framework — and then a platform — designed to make resilience science accessible to everyone.

Janeen observed that most resilience tools treated resilience as a single trait, or focused only on one domain — psychology, spirituality, or physical wellness. The reality, her research revealed, is that resilience is multidimensional: a constellation of capacities that interact in complex and personal ways.

The Resilience Atlas was created to reflect that complexity — giving people a map, not just a number, for understanding how they adapt under pressure.

Our Mission

"To make the science of resilience legible, personal, and actionable — so that every person can understand, cultivate, and share their capacity to grow through adversity."

We believe resilience is not something you either have or don't have. It is a set of learnable, developable capacities — and the first step is understanding where you already are and where you have room to grow.

The Framework

The Six Dimensions of Resilience framework integrates findings from multiple scientific traditions. Each dimension reflects a distinct way that humans adapt to and recover from adversity:

🤝 Relational — social support and connection 🧠 Cognitive — reframing and perspective-taking 💚 Somatic — physical regulation and body awareness 💕 Emotional — processing and moving through feelings 🌿 Spiritual — meaning, purpose, and values ⚡ Agentic — action, agency, and forward movement

The atlas metaphor is intentional: a map doesn't tell you where to go — it helps you understand where you are and what paths are available. The Resilience Atlas does the same for your inner life.

Research Origins

The Resilience Atlas framework is grounded in peer-reviewed research across:

Positive Psychology Resilience Science Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT) Cross-Cultural Research Trauma-Informed Practice

The assessment instrument was developed through doctoral research and refined through iterative testing to ensure it reflects the complexity of real human resilience.
