Workshop Guide

Relational-Connective

A facilitated team session to explore and strengthen Relational-Connective resilience

⏱ 60–90 minutes 👥 5–25 participants 🎯 Team development

Overview

Relational-Connective resilience refers to the capacity to build, maintain, and draw strength from meaningful human connections. Teams with strong relational resilience navigate adversity together, supporting each other through difficulty and finding renewal in shared experience.

This guide provides a structured facilitation framework including discussion prompts, activities, and reflection questions. Adapt the timing and sequence to suit your team's needs.

Facilitator note: Create a psychologically safe space before starting. Remind participants that sharing is always voluntary and that there are no right or wrong answers.

Discussion Prompts

Choose 3–5 prompts that feel most relevant to your team. Allow 5–8 minutes per prompt for open discussion.

  1. When do you feel most connected to your teammates — and what creates that feeling?
  2. What gets in the way of asking for support when you need it most?
  3. How do we acknowledge and celebrate each other's contributions as a team?
  4. What does belonging feel like in our team culture?
  5. Who in your network outside this team do you lean on, and do you have enough of those connections?
  6. What's one small thing we could do this week to feel more connected as a team?
  7. When someone is struggling, how do we notice and respond as a team?

Team Activities

Select one or two activities depending on your available time.

  • Connection Map (15 min): Each person draws their relational network. Share in pairs: Where are your strongest connections? Where are there gaps?
  • Appreciation Circle (10 min): Go around the team — each person shares one specific thing they appreciate about the person to their left.
  • Support Menu (20 min): Each team member writes their personal "support menu" — what kind of help they find most useful. Share and discuss.

Reflection Questions

Close the session with individual reflection (5 min silent writing) followed by brief sharing.

  • What surprised you about your team's relational strengths and gaps?
  • What is one thing you will do differently this week to strengthen a team connection?
  • How can we build connection into our team rituals and routines?

Next Steps

After the workshop, capture commitments and schedule a follow-up check-in:

  • Each person identifies one concrete action they'll take before the next check-in.
  • The team agrees on one shared practice to implement together.
  • Schedule a 15-minute check-in in 2–4 weeks to review progress.
  • Consider reassessing your team's resilience profile in 30 days to track growth.

Track progress: Return to your Team Dashboard after your next assessment cycle to see how your scores have shifted.
