Workshop Guide

Somatic-Behavioral

A facilitated team session to explore and strengthen Somatic-Behavioral resilience

⏱ 60–90 minutes 👥 5–25 participants 🎯 Team development

Overview

Somatic-Behavioral resilience refers to physical self-regulation, body awareness, and the ability to ground oneself under stress. Teams with high somatic resilience understand their physical stress signals and have practices to restore balance and vitality.

This guide provides a structured facilitation framework including discussion prompts, activities, and reflection questions. Adapt the timing and sequence to suit your team's needs.

Facilitator note: Create a psychologically safe space before starting. Remind participants that sharing is always voluntary and that there are no right or wrong answers.

Discussion Prompts

Choose 3–5 prompts that feel most relevant to your team. Allow 5–8 minutes per prompt for open discussion.

  1. How do you know when you're in a stress response vs. a grounded, resourced state?
  2. What physical signals tell you that you — or a teammate — are approaching burnout?
  3. How does your physical environment (light, noise, movement) affect your focus and wellbeing?
  4. What collective habits support your physical wellbeing as a team?
  5. How do we create space for recovery and rest — not just productivity?
  6. What's one simple body-based practice we could add to our team rituals?
  7. How do we respond as a team when someone's energy is depleted?

Team Activities

Select one or two activities depending on your available time.

  • Body Scan (10 min): Guide the team through a brief body scan. Ask: Where do you hold tension? What does your body need right now?
  • Movement Break Design (15 min): As a team, design a 3-minute movement break protocol you'll use in meetings. Test it together.
  • Stress Signals Map (20 min): Each person identifies their personal stress signals. In pairs, share what you'd want colleagues to notice and how to respond helpfully.

Reflection Questions

Close the session with individual reflection (5 min silent writing) followed by brief sharing.

  • What is your body telling you about your current level of resilience?
  • What one physical practice will you commit to this week to support your wellbeing?
  • How can the team create more space for physical recovery and rest?

Next Steps

After the workshop, capture commitments and schedule a follow-up check-in:

  • Each person identifies one concrete action they'll take before the next check-in.
  • The team agrees on one shared practice to implement together.
  • Schedule a 15-minute check-in in 2–4 weeks to review progress.
  • Consider reassessing your team's resilience profile in 30 days to track growth.

Track progress: Return to your Team Dashboard after your next assessment cycle to see how your scores have shifted.
