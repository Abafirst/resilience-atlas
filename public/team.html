Facilitation

Team Building Activities

Structured activities you can run in team meetings, workshops, or off-sites to build resilience skills together.

🎯
Dimension Deep-Dive
45–60 min 4–20 people All Dimensions

Each team member shares which of the Six Dimensions feels strongest and which is a current growth edge. The group identifies patterns and creates a collective resilience profile together.

How to run it:
  1. Each person picks their strongest and weakest dimension from the Six Dimensions wheel (5 min alone).
  2. In pairs, share your choices and the reason why (10 min).
  3. Pairs share back to the group — facilitator captures themes on a whiteboard (15 min).
  4. Discuss: What does our collective strength look like? Where are our shared growth edges? (15 min).
  5. Each person names one action they'll take to strengthen their growth dimension this month (10 min).

Materials: Six Dimensions wheel diagram, sticky notes, whiteboard or shared doc.

💬
Resilience Story Circle
30–45 min 5–15 people All Dimensions

Team members share brief stories of navigating a professional challenge. The group identifies which resilience dimension was most active — building empathy, insight, and shared language.

How to run it:
  1. Facilitator introduces the Six Dimensions briefly (5 min).
  2. Each person thinks of a time they navigated something hard at work and came through it (2 min alone).
  3. Volunteers share their story (2–3 min each). After each, the group calls out which dimension they heard most.
  4. Debrief: What patterns did we notice? What surprised us? (10 min).
  5. Close: Each person names one resilience strength they want to lean on this quarter.

Tip: No story is too small. The point is recognition, not drama.

📈
Team Strengths Mapping
30 min 3–12 people All Dimensions

Using individual assessment results, plot the team's collective dimension scores on a shared radar chart. Identify team strengths and coverage gaps — then assign roles accordingly.

How to run it:
  1. Display the team's aggregated radar chart from the dashboard (5 min).
  2. Ask: Where is the team strong? Where is the lowest score? (5 min discussion).
  3. Explore: How does this map to how we actually work together? Are gaps causing friction? (10 min).
  4. Identify: Who on the team has natural strength in each dimension? How can we use that? (10 min).

Requires: Teams Pro or Enterprise tier for aggregated dashboard access.

☁
The Pressure Test
45 min 6–20 people Emotional-Adaptive

Teams explore how pressure affects performance and emotional regulation. They identify their collective early warning signs and build a shared "pressure protocol" for high-stakes moments.

How to run it:
  1. Intro: Share research on how stress affects decision-making and communication (5 min).
  2. Individual reflection: "When I'm under pressure, I tend to..." — write silently (5 min).
  3. Small groups of 3–4 share their pressure signatures (10 min).
  4. Whole group: What pressure patterns did we identify across the team? (10 min).
  5. Co-create a "pressure protocol" — agreed-upon team norms for high-stress moments (15 min).

Tip: Keep the tone non-judgmental. Pressure responses are normal — the goal is awareness, not change.

🧭
Values Alignment Workshop
60 min 4–15 people Spiritual-Reflective

Team members clarify their personal top values and compare with the team's stated values. The group identifies alignment, tensions, and opportunities — and commits to shared working principles.

How to run it:
  1. Each person lists their top 5 personal values (from a provided list or free-form) — 5 min.
  2. In pairs: share your top 3 and why — 10 min.
  3. Facilitator compiles a collective "value word cloud" on the whiteboard — 5 min.
  4. Compare with team/company stated values: Where do we align? Where is there tension? — 15 min.
  5. Draft 3–5 team working principles that reflect shared values — 20 min.
  6. Commit: How will we hold each other to these? — 5 min.

Output: A team values charter or working agreement to post and revisit quarterly.

👥
Connection Map
30 min 4–20 people Relational-Connective

Teams visually map their internal connections, support networks, and collaboration patterns. They identify who might feel isolated, who is over-relied upon, and what gaps need bridging.

How to run it:
  1. Each person draws a simple map with their name in the center. They draw lines to team members they connect with regularly, with thicker lines = stronger connection (10 min).
  2. Share maps in small groups (3–4 people). What do you notice? (10 min).
  3. Debrief with the whole group: Who might need more connection? Who is a key connector? Are there silos? (10 min).

Tip: This works especially well for remote or hybrid teams. Use a digital whiteboard like Miro or FigJam.

Visual Guide

The Six Dimensions of Resilience

A visual reference for teams: what each dimension looks like in action, and what to watch for.

Relational-Connective
The Connector

The ability to build and sustain meaningful connections, ask for help, and offer support. Connectors create the safety nets that hold teams together under stress.

Signals of strength:
  • Proactively checks in with colleagues
  • Comfortable asking for and accepting help
  • Creates psychological safety in meetings
  • Builds bridges across silos
Cognitive-Narrative
The Thinker

The ability to reframe challenges, shift perspective, and construct adaptive narratives. Thinkers help teams find meaning in setbacks and possibilities in constraints.

Signals of strength:
  • Reframes problems into opportunities
  • Considers multiple perspectives
  • Maintains nuanced thinking under pressure
  • Challenges unhelpful team narratives
Somatic-Regulative
The Grounder

The capacity to regulate the body's stress response and maintain physical equilibrium. Grounders model calm under pressure and help teams return to baseline quickly.

Signals of strength:
  • Physically calm in high-stakes moments
  • Recovers quickly from stress peaks
  • Aware of how physical state affects performance
  • Practices intentional restoration
Emotional-Adaptive
The Feeler

The ability to name, understand, and work skillfully with emotions — one's own and others'. Feelers create emotional intelligence in team culture and prevent burnout.

Signals of strength:
  • Names emotions clearly and non-reactively
  • Reads the emotional tone of meetings
  • Responds thoughtfully rather than reactively
  • Helps teams process and move forward
Spiritual-Reflective
The Guide

The capacity to connect with meaning, purpose, and values — especially during difficulty. Guides help teams stay anchored to what matters most when everything feels uncertain.

Signals of strength:
  • Articulates the deeper "why" behind work
  • Makes decisions aligned with core values
  • Brings perspective during crises
  • Helps teams find meaning in hard moments
Agentic-Generative
The Builder

The belief in one's capacity to act effectively and create change. Builders drive initiative, maintain momentum under uncertainty, and inspire action-orientation in others.

Signals of strength:
  • Takes initiative without waiting for permission
  • Breaks challenges into actionable steps
  • Maintains momentum through setbacks
  • Energizes others toward possibility
