The Framework

The Six Dimensions of Resilience

Resilience is not a single trait. It is a constellation of capacities. Each dimension reflects a distinct way you adapt, recover, and grow.

Relational-Connective

How you access and draw on support from others. Your capacity to maintain connections and seek help when facing adversity.

Cognitive-Narrative

How you interpret and reframe challenges. Your ability to shift perspective, find meaning, and construct a narrative of growth.

Somatic-Regulative

How your body regulates stress. Your physiological capacity to move through tension, restore calm, and sustain energy.

Emotional-Adaptive

How you process difficult feelings. Your capacity to acknowledge, experience, and move through challenging emotional states.

Spiritual-Reflective

How meaning and purpose shape resilience. Your connection to values, beliefs, and a larger sense of why that sustains you.

Agentic-Generative

How you take action in adversity. Your capacity to move forward, make decisions, and assert agency even in uncertainty.

Why It Matters

Why Resilience Has Multiple Dimensions

A single resilience score misses the complexity of how people actually respond under pressure. The six-dimension model gives you actionable, nuanced insight.

No single path to resilience

People recover and adapt through different pathways — some through connection, others through meaning, action, or physical regulation.

Strengths and growth edges

Knowing which dimensions are strong and which need development gives you a targeted, personalized roadmap rather than generic advice.

Contextual resilience

Different challenges activate different dimensions. Understanding your profile prepares you for the full range of life's adversities.

Science-informed design

Each dimension is grounded in resilience research, positive psychology, somatic science, and clinical practice.
